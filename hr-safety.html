HR & Safety Professionals Day

Join us for a one-day event dedicated to bringing human resources and safety professionals together to share best practices and resources for the good of the Houston commercial construction market.

Wednesday, August 24, 2022 from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM
Constellation Field

Keynote Speaker

Sponsored By
What to expect

Presentations

Facility Security: Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design

Summary

Are you ready to manage a critical event? This program will walk us through how we can learn to secure your building using the CPTED model (Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design). This program is an introduction to workplace design assessment and usage for vulnerability, security, and crime prevention and will cover best practices, policies, and procedures that every facility should have in place.

Presenter
Rick Hernandez
Safety Director, Gamma Construction

For the past nine years, Rick has provided civilians the chance to defend themselves while he instructs concealed handgun licensing and firearms training courses. Rick currently serves as a safety professional with Gamma Construction located in Houston, Texas. After proudly serving his country for four years overseas as a Navy Petty Officer (Gunners Mate), he went on to protect and serve those locally back in Texas. While in the Navy, he courageously fought in Operation Desert Storm, thus becoming a life member of the Veterans of Foreign Wars.

Total Rewards: Attract, Retain and Grow Your Biggest Assets

Summary

A total rewards programs in the workplace includes all forms or monetary and non-monetary compensation and benefits offered to employees. Total rewards programs are effective tools that employers can utilize when facing the daunting tasks of attracting and keeping employees. In this session we will hear from three professionals who will discuss elements of their company’s total rewards programs and leave you with best practices and strategies for creating and implementing your career development packages.

Presenters
Shannon Davis
Talent Development Manager, Marek Brothers Systems, Inc.

Shannon Davis is the Talent Development Manager for MAREK, an 85-year construction contractor recognized for its deep commitment to growing people throughout operations in Texas and the Southeast. Shannon and her team partner with leadership to design and deliver solutions that help them achieve their goals. She plays a key role in driving high organizational performance through attracting and developing exceptional talent.

Melissa Bargainer
Human Resources Manager, Tellepsen

Melissa is the Human Resources Manger with Tellepsen builders. Prior to joining Tellepsen she was the Vice President Resource Management and Operations at Hewlett-Packard. She has Significant experience outside HR function in Operations roles with deep understanding of labor strategies, performance management, talent management and organization development.

Pamela Ames
HR Business Partner, TDIndustries, Inc.

Pam Ames, SPHR, is the Sr. Manager, Human Resources for TDIndustries. She has over 20 years of experience, with the last 3.5 at TDIndustries. Pam started her career working in non-profit organizations and has worked in hospitality, facilities management, healthcare, and construction industries. She enjoys facilitating training courses and helping leaders to grow. Pam has her bachelor’s degree from Texas State University. She has her Sr. Professional in Human Resources certification.

Crisis and Emergency Communications: I Love It When A Plan Comes Together

Summary

Being prepared to respond to an incident or crisis with a modern, effective, and comprehensive plan is critical for any company to safeguard their employees, operation, and their reputation. How would your team respond in a crisis? How will you make your plan come together when it matters?

Presenters
John Marshall
Senior Vice President, Satterfield & Pontikes Construction, Inc.

Prior to joining S&P in 2002, John was a global director for the Fluor Corporation, where he also headed a strategically important pilot study of world-class business and competitive intelligence development. Before that, he was a staff member for the US House of Representatives, Committee on Rules. John holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from Boise State University and is currently serving a two-year term as Chairman of the Board of the Houston AGC.

Daniel Wagstaff
CEO, Pocketstop

As the CEO of Pocketstop, Daniel Wagstaff defines the strategic direction of the business while identifying and managing key business opportunities. He founded the company to focus on solving an organization’s largest communications issues by using proven technologies in unique ways ensuring solutions are simple, efficient, and delivering measurable business results. Daniel has 20 years of diverse experience working with clients to ensure their B2B and B2C communication strategy motivates and drives the desired behavior. Before Pocketstop, Daniel worked at Aerotek, the largest private recruiting firm in the world, leading business operations.

Experience Modifiers 101: Workers' Comp Modifiers Who, What, When, Where & Why

Summary

Get the rationale behind the math of work comp modifiers and how the numbers impact your company. Review what steps can your company take to mitigate risk and manage losses that could result in higher premiums and loss of work.

Presenter
Julie K. Schatz
Program Administrator, CompGroup AGC

Julie has been involved in the CompGroup AGC since its initial policyholder was written back in March 1993. She has managed the program throughout these years as Senior Vice President at Roberts & Crow, Inc. Julie has been in insurance since 1982, initially as a customer service representative, moving into sales as an independent agent and now specializing in risk management services. She is a Certified Insurance Counselor and a Certified Risk Manager.

How to Create a Companywide Mental Health Program

Summary

We will discuss the intersection and integration of HR, Safety, and mental health, in the context of the mental health crisis in the construction industry. Construction leads all industries in suicide. Suicide has now eclipsed the “fatal 4” in terms of lives lost in this industry. Simply, this is a topic that can no longer be an outlier discussed once a year. These session objectives will be to educate HR & Training Professionals on the importance of discussing mental health issues in the workplace and implementing a more robust resiliency training program within our organizations that is interwoven through our existing Health & Safety programs.

Presenters
Dr. Ryan Todd
CEO, Headversity

Dr. Ryan Todd is a psychiatrist in the blue-collar city of Calgary, Alberta. Where he has seen and treated thousands of individuals who have a mental health issue as the result of workplace trauma. Dr. Todd believes mental wellness can be learned and practiced by everyone, and that technology is a vital resource in helping bridge the gap between demand and mental healthcare today. This belief fueled his desire to start Headversity, a mental wellness tech company where he is the CEO.

Dr. Alejandra "Ally" Sequeira
Owner, Houston OCD & Anxiety

Dr. Alejandra "Ally" Sequeira is a psychologist, licensed in Texas, California, and through PSYCHPACT. She obtained her doctorate in Counseling Psychology and Master of Education in Educational Psychology from Texas A&M University. She is the owner of Houston OCD & Anxiety where she provides individualized, evidence-based treatment for children, adolescents, and adults with OCD, OCD-related disorders, anxiety disorders, disordered eating, depression, parenting-related challenges, and other life stressors in both English and Spanish. She serves as the Secretary of the Houston Psychological Association Board and is a board member of OCD Texas.

Marijuana in the Construction Industry: A Joint Discussion

Summary

Since the legalization of marijuana in some states across the country there has been renewed and increased interest in the issues surrounding drug testing, safety culture, and employment policies. This program will use a panel of construction industry professionals to discuss current considerations and issues plaquing the construction industry.

Presenters
Anthony Stergio
Andrews Myers, PC

Board Certified in Labor and Employment Law by the Texas Board of Legal Specialization, Anthony G. “Tony” Stergio has extensive experience in the defense of State and Federal employment discrimination claims, wage and hour compliance and claims, OSHA compliance and contests, Davis-Bacon Act compliance, noncompetition agreements, Department of Labor and TWC investigations and employment policy design and review. He speaks frequently at employment-related seminars and also counsels clients regarding developments in various areas of State and Federal employment law.

In 2017, Tony received an AV© Preeminent™ Judicial Rating from Martindale Hubbell, the nation’s leading legal services resource. He scored a 5 out of 5 rating, the highest rating achievable in both Legal Ability and Ethical Standards. He previously received an AV© Preeminent™ Peer Review Rating from Martindale Hubbell in 2011.

Bobby Skyles
Marek Brothers Systems, Inc.

Bobby has been with MAREK for 27 years and has over 18 years of HR experience. In his current role as Director of Human Resources, he leads the HR function for MAREK.

Tommy Lee
W.S. Bellows Construction Corp.

Tommy Lee joined Bellows in 1981 and has since set the standard regarding safety for our company, our employees, and the industry. His leadership and continuous development of innovative safety programs has earned him the title of Vice President of Safety.

Tommy oversees and manages the Bellows safety program for every job and is dedicated to the safety of our clients and personnel. Tommy has led Bellows in receiving First Place at the National Associated General Contractors of America Excellence in Safety Awards in 2011, 2014, 2017, and 2020 and the Excellence in Safety Grand Award in 2017. Tommy earned a Bachelor of Science in Industrial Technology from the University of Houston.
